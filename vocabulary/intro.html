🧠 Easy Notes

  • Context clues are hints in the sentence that help you guess a word's meaning.
  • Look for words like "because," "so," or "but" — they give clues!
  • If you see a hard word, read the whole sentence again slowly.
  • Sometimes the sentence before or after explains the word.
  • Replace the hard word with each answer choice — does it make sense?
  • Watch for opposite meanings with words like "unlike" or "however."
  • A synonym nearby often means the same thing as the tricky word.
  • Don't panic if you don't know the word — the story usually helps you!
  • Picture the scene in your head. It makes guessing easier.
  • Take your time. Rushing makes you miss the clues!
  • If two answers seem close, pick the one that fits the feeling of the story.
  • Practice every day — your vocabulary grows like a muscle!

Vocabulary in Context

Learn new words by reading between the lines.

What is Vocabulary in Context?

Vocabulary in context means figuring out what a word means by looking at the words and sentences around it. You do not need a dictionary — the story gives you clues!

Strong readers use context clues to unlock meaning. They notice synonyms, antonyms, explanations, and examples hidden right in the paragraph.

Example Passage

The old bridge was so fragile that the town closed it to cars. Even a small truck might make the wooden beams crack and splinter.

Two Kinds of Context Clues

Authors help readers in two main ways:

  • Direct Explanation: The meaning is spelled out nearby.
  • Indirect Hint: You must infer from the situation.

Direct Example: What does fragile mean?

Answer: Easy to break — "crack and splinter" tells you directly.

Indirect Example: Why did the town close the bridge?

Answer: They were worried it would break. — not said directly, but strongly suggested.
