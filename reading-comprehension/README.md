# Reading Comprehension Module

A self-contained, interactive learning module for students to practice reading comprehension. No installation, no internet, no accounts — just open and learn.

---

## 🚀 How to Set Up

Follow these simple steps to get started:

1. **Download the folder** as a `.zip` file from the source.
2. **Extract** the `.zip` file to your computer (right-click → Extract All).
3. **Open `intro.html` first** by double-clicking it. It will open in your web browser.
4. Read the introduction, then click **"Start the Quiz"** to begin the practice quiz.

> **Tip for Teachers & Parents:** The entire module works offline. Save the folder to a USB drive or your desktop for easy access anytime.

---

## 🎓 5 Tips for Students

1. **Read slowly.** Take your time with the passage. Understanding beats speed every time.
2. **Look back at the story.** Most answers are hiding right there in the text — you just need to find them.
3. **Think before you click.** Try answering the question in your head before looking at the choices.
4. **Learn from mistakes.** If you get one wrong, read the feedback and try to see why. That is how you grow!
5. **Take breaks.** If your brain feels tired, pause and come back later. Learning works best when you are fresh.

---

## 🍎 5 Tips for Teachers & Homeschool Parents

1. **Start with the intro.** Always have students open `intro.html` first. It teaches the difference between explicit and implicit questions before the quiz begins.
2. **Use it offline.** No internet? No problem. This module works entirely from the folder after extraction — perfect for classrooms with limited connectivity.
3. **Encourage self-pacing.** Let students work at their own speed. The instant feedback per question means you do not need to hover — they learn independently.
4. **Discuss the "why."** After the quiz, ask students to explain why an answer was right or wrong. This deepens understanding beyond just the score.
5. **Repeat for mastery.** Students can retake the quiz as many times as they like (`Try Again` button). Use it for review, warm-ups, or homework practice.
