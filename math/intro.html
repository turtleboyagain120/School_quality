🧮 Math Tips

  • Decimals show parts of a whole. 0.3 = 3 tenths.
  • Count places after the dot: tenths, hundredths.
  • Mixed numbers have a whole number + fraction.
  • 2 1/2 means 2 wholes + half more.
  • Line up decimal points when adding.
  • Draw pictures for fractions - helps visualize!
  • 3 2/4 simplifies to 3 1/2.
  • 0.5 is the same as 1/2.
  • Read decimals aloud: 0.23 is "twenty-three hundredths."
  • Mixed to improper: 2 1/2 = (2×2 + 1)/2 = 5/2.
  • Always check: does my answer make sense?
  • Practice daily - math gets easier with reps!

Decimals & Mixed Numbers

Learn to read, understand, and work with decimals and mixed numbers.

What are Decimals?

Decimals show parts of a whole. The digits after the decimal point tell tenths, hundredths, etc.

Examples:

  • 0.3 = 3 tenths (3 out of 10)
  • 0.25 = 25 hundredths (25 out of 100)

This shows 0.3.

What are Mixed Numbers?

A mixed number combines a whole number and a fraction.
